近视防控-近视背后的科学：波长(Wavelength)双语

2020-11-14
| 科技

​During emmetropization, the eye needs some sort of visual cue to determine the direction of blur, and chromatic aberrations have been suggested as a possible cue. The most common chromatic aberration is longitudinal chromatic aberration (LCA), in which different wavelengths of light are focussed at differentdistances along the same axis (Figure 11).

在人眼正视化过程中，眼睛需要某种视觉方式来确定模糊的方向，色差被认为是可能的方式。最常见的色差是纵向色差(LCA)，即不同波长的光在同一轴上焦距不同。

注：色差（又称为“色散现象”）是由于透镜没有把不同波长的光线聚焦到同一个焦平面（不同波长的光线的焦距是不同的），或者和透镜对不同波长的光线放大的程度不同而形成的。色差又可分为“纵向色差”和“横向色差”，纵向色差：不同颜色光线的波长不同，焦距也不同；横向色差：不同颜色光线的波长不同，放大倍率不同。

Figure 11. Longitudinal chromatic aberration is due to wavelength-dependent differences in refraction of light by the same lens. Light of shorter wavelengths, which appears violet to blue-green, is refracted more strongly than light of longer wavelength, such as that which appears yellow-green or red, and consequently is focused closer to the lens.

图11。纵向色差是由于同一透镜折射光的波长差异造成的。波长较短的光，即从紫色到蓝绿色的光，比波长较长的光，如黄绿色或红色的光折射更强，因此更靠近透镜。

In humans, the fovea is most densely populated with medium- and long-wavelength-sensitive cones. One idea is that the eye should be guided to grow to a size at which middle wavelengths in the visible spectrum (yellow- green, 550-570nm) will be the most sharply focussed, with the highest contrast, to attain the highest possible spatial acuity. If this were the case, the in-focus image of an eye that is too small will be blue-shifted, with high contrast at short-wavelengths (450-470 nm), whereas the in-focus image of an eye that is too large will be red- shifted with high contrast at long-wavelengths (610-630 nm) (Figure 12).

人眼中央凹对长波长和中波长敏感的视锥细胞密度最大。一种观点是，引导眼球发育到可见光谱中的中波(黄-绿，550-570nm)聚焦最锐利，对比度最高，能达到最高的空间敏锐度。按此观点, 高对比度在短波长(450 - 470nm)的小图像, 眼睛的焦距会蓝移, 而高对比度的长波长(610 - 630 nm)大图像，眼睛的焦距会红移(图12)。

Figure 12. One hypothesis about the effect of LCA on defocus and contrast. In an emmetropic eye (a), the green region of the spectrum (middle-wavelengths) would be the most in-focus and have the highest contrast. If the eye is too short (b), the blue region of the spectrum (short-wavelengths) will be the most in-focus and have the highest contrast. If the eye is too long (c), the red region spectrum (long-wavelengths) will be most in-focus and have the highest contrast.

图12.关于LCA对散焦和对比度影响的一种假设。在正视眼（a）中，光谱的绿色区域（中波）将最聚焦，并且对比度最高。如果眼睛太短（b），则光谱的蓝色区域（短波长）将最聚焦，并且对比度最高。如果眼睛太长（c），红色区域的光谱（长波长）将最聚焦并且对比度最高。

The results of research on the effects of LCA on emmetropization in the eye show that it is sufficient to detect the direction of retinal blur, but it does not seem to be necessary for emmetropization. Despite this, many studies have demonstrated clearly that the chromaticity (spectral composition) of light can be an important modulator of eye growth, although its effects do not seem to be the same in all animal models. In chickens raised without goggles or lenses, short-wavelength (blue) light retards eye growth, whereas long- wavelength (red) light promotes it; white light (which contains all spectral wavelengths) has no effect. In chicks wearing monocular diffusers, long-wavelength illumination (achieved using filters) is myopia-promoting, while short-wavelength illumination is myopia-inhibiting. In contrast, long-wavelength light is growth-inhibiting and short-wavelength light is growth-promoting in rhesus monkey and tree shrew. Thus, further investigations are needed to explore the effects of light spectrum on myopia development and to identify the underlying retinal mechanisms, in various animal models.

关于LCA对眼睛的正视化作用的影响的研究结果表明，它足以检测出视网膜模糊的方向，但似乎对正视化没有必要。尽管如此，许多研究清楚地表明，光的色度（光谱组成）可能是眼睛生长的重要调节剂，尽管在所有动物模型中其作用似乎都不相同。在没有护目镜或镜片的情况下饲养的鸡，短波长（蓝色）的光会阻碍眼睛的生长，而长波长（红色）的光会促进眼睛的生长。白光（包含所有光谱波长）无效。在戴单眼漫射器的雏鸡中，长波长照明（使用滤光片可达到）可促进近视，而短波长照明可抑制近视。相反，在恒河猴和树鼩中，长波长光抑制生长，而短波长光则促进生长。因此，在各种动物模型中，需要进一步的研究来探索光谱对近视发展的影响并确定潜在的机制。
