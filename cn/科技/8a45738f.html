盘点哪些损失惨重的意外

2020-11-14
| 科技

点击蓝字｜关注我们

总有一些意外防不胜防，总有一些事故家破人亡。每年世界上都会发生数以万计的意外，小的意外造成的结果只是到擦伤脱皮，大的造成数亿元的损失。今天就来盘点一下19世纪以来造成损失最大的7次意外，这里只记直接损失。意外不包括战争、金融危机、以及自然灾害。

Top7：泰坦尼克沉没，损失1.5亿美元。

这艘号称“永不沉没”的邮轮在第一次的航行的时刻就惨遭打脸。1912年4月14日23时40分左右，泰坦尼克号与一座冰山相撞，最终船体沉没，至今仍未被打捞上来，造成损失约为1.5亿美元，1517人丧生。

Top6：加利福尼亚火车事件，损失5亿美元.

2008年9月12日在加利福尼亚，MetroLink 城郊客运火车与一列洛杉矶开过来的货运车相撞，25人丧生。

Top5：ExxonValdez油轮泄漏损失30亿美元.

埃克森瓦尔德斯原油泄漏是美国国内最严重的原油泄漏事故。事故造成了大约3千万加仑（115,000 m3）原油泄漏，事后Exxon动用了11,000名工人花费了大约20亿美元进行原油清理工作， 并且另外花费了10亿美元解决相关民事赔偿。

Top4、挑战号航天飞机爆炸，损失55亿美元.

1986年1月28日，美国挑战号航天飞机在发射后73秒爆炸，原因是一个O型密封圈失职导致高压气体外泄然后主燃料槽的液化氢跟它反应之从而引发巨大的爆炸。更换航天飞机花费45亿，调研和其它设备维护费用花了10亿（折算后）。

Top3、Prestige 号漏油，损失120亿美元.

　2002年11月13日，运载了7万7千吨原油的 Prestige 号油轮在西班牙的加利西亚遭遇风暴导致12个油罐中的1个爆炸，船长担心沉船于是向西班牙求助，希望能让 Prestige 号入港。但是当地政府最终没有同意，最后船体被风暴毁成两截，2千万加仑的原油倾入大海。

Top2、哥伦比亚号航天飞机事故，损失130亿美元.

这次事故直接加速了美国航天飞机的退役，此次事件的原因是航天飞机外部燃料箱表面一块隔热材料脱落并击中航天飞机机翼造成破损。事故的总成本包括调查取证和残骸搜救等，共花费130亿美元。

Top1、切尔诺贝利，2000亿美元.

发生于1986年4月26日的切尔诺贝利核泄漏事件是和平时期最严重的人类浩劫，它让整个乌克兰50%的地区受污染，170万人受到直接影响，20万人被迫撤离，之后因癌症死亡的人数估计约12万5千人，事后造成的损失高达2000亿美元。

Today's article

There are always accidents that cannot be prevented, and there are always accidents. Tens of thousands of accidents happen in the world every year. Small accidents only cause bruises and peeling, and large ones cause hundreds of millions of dollars in losses. Today, let’s take a look at the 7 accidents that caused the biggest losses since the 19th century. Only direct losses are recorded here. Accidents do not include wars, financial crises, and natural disasters.


Top7: Titanic sank and lost 150 million US dollars.

This so-called "unsinkable" cruise ship was slapped in the face at the moment of its first voyage. At around 23:40 on April 14, 1912, the Titanic collided with an iceberg, and the hull eventually sank. It has not been salvaged yet, causing losses of approximately US$150 million and 1,517 lives.


Top6: The California train collision incident, the loss of 500 million US dollars.

On September 12, 2008, in California, a MetroLink suburban passenger train collided with a freight car coming from Los Angeles, killing 25 people.


Top5: The Exxon Valdez tanker leaked and lost USD 3 billion.

The Exxon Valdes crude oil spill is the most serious crude oil spill in the United States. The accident caused approximately 30 million gallons (115,000 m3) of crude oil to leak. Afterwards, Exxon used 11,000 workers to spend approximately US$2 billion on crude oil clean-up work and another US$1 billion to settle related civil compensation.


Top4. The space shuttle Challenge exploded and lost US$5.5 billion.

On January 28, 1986, the U.S. Space Shuttle Challenge exploded 73 seconds after launch. The cause was that an O-ring was negligent and caused high-pressure gas to leak out. Then the liquefied hydrogen in the main fuel tank reacted with it and caused a huge explosion. It cost 4.5 billion to replace the space shuttle, and 1 billion (after conversion) for research and maintenance of other equipment.


Top3, Prestige oil spill, loss of 12 billion US dollars.

On November 13, 2002, the oil tanker Prestige, carrying 77,000 tons of crude oil, encountered a storm in Galicia, Spain, causing one of 12 oil tanks to explode. The captain was worried about the sinking of the ship and asked Spain for help, hoping to make Prestige. Number of arrival. However, the local government finally did not agree. In the end, the hull was destroyed in two by the storm, and 20 million gallons of crude oil was poured into the sea.


Top2: The Columbia Space Shuttle accident, with a loss of $13 billion.

This accident directly accelerated the retirement of the US space shuttle. The cause of this incident was that a piece of insulating material on the outer fuel tank surface of the space shuttle fell off and hit the space shuttle wing, causing damage. The total cost of the accident, including investigation and evidence collection and wreck search and rescue, etc., cost a total of 13 billion US dollars.


Top1, Chernobyl, 200 billion US dollars.

The Chernobyl nuclear spill that occurred on April 26, 1986 was the worst human catastrophe in peace. It polluted 50% of the entire Ukraine. 1.7 million people were directly affected and 200,000 people were forced to evacuate. After that, the number of deaths due to cancer is estimated to be about 125,000, and the loss caused afterwards is as high as 200 billion US dollars.
