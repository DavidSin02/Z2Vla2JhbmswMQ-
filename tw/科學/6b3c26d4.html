Dislocations can greatly enhance the figure of merit of thermoelectric materials by prominently reducing thermal conductivity. However, the evolution of phonon modes with different energies when they propagate through a single dislocation is unknown. Here we perform non-equilibrium molecular dynamics simulation to study phonon transport in PbTe crystal with dislocations by excluding boundary scattering and strain coupling effect. The frequency-dependent heat flux, phonon mode analysis, and frequency-dependent phonon mean free paths (MFPs) are presented. The thermal conductivity of PbTe with dislocation density on the order of 1015mâˆ’2 is decreased by 62%. We provide solid evidence of strong localization of phonon modes in dislocation sample. These results provide insightful guidance for the development of PbTe based thermoelectrics and shed light on new routes for enhancing the performance of existing thermoelectrics by incorporating dislocations.
