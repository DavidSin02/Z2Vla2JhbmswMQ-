Caramel Pecan Entremet

by Kevin Gully

Caramel glaze

150g water

200g liquid glucose (A)

300g caster (superfine) sugar

100g liquid glucose (B)

200g evaporated milk

160g gelatine mass (1:6)

280g Callebaut gold couverture 30.4%

20g Callebaut Arriba milk couverture 39%

150g mirror glaze

METHOD

Heat the water and glucose (A) in a saucepan, whisking occasionally by hand. Once hot, remove the saucepan from the heat and set aside.

Heat the sugar and glucose (B) in a separate saucepan and stir occasionally with a spatula to make a caramel. When the caramel is ready (at 180 ËšC to 185ËšC), remove from the heat and slowly pour in the hot water with the glucose in intervals, while whisking by hand. Be careful of the steam. When all the water has been added, pour in the evaporated milk. Return to the heat and bring to a quick boil. As it reaches a boil, remove from the heat and whisk in the gelatine mass. Once it is combined, pour it over the gold chocolate and milk chocolate in a bowl and whisk by hand to combine. Add the mirror glaze and emulsify with a stick blender until smooth. Please note, the glaze may split a little due to the condensed milk, but will retrieve itself when emulsifying. Cover with plastic wrap touching the surface of the glaze and leave in the fridge overnight.


Gluten free flour mix

300g buckwheat flour

100g rice flour

100g cornflour/ corn starch

METHOD

Place all the ingredients in a bowl and whisk together by hand. Once combined, sift onto a sheet of baking paper.

Weigh out 45g for the recipe below and store the remainder in an airtight container for another recipe.

Caramel sponge

100g caster (superfine) sugar

40g unsalted butter

50g hot water

70g pecan powder

45g gluten free flour mix (above)

50g egg yolks

75g egg white

75g inverted sugar

50g pecans, for sprinkling

METHOD

To create the pecan powder, roast some pecans in the oven and grind in a blender to create a powder. Weigh out 140g for this recipe and set the remaining aside for use later in the recipe.

Heat the sugar in a saucepan to make a caramel. Add the butter and slowly pour in the hot water, being careful of the hot steam. Whisk by hand to combine. Place the pecan powder and gluten free flour mix in a bowl and pour in the caramel, whisking together by hand. Allow to sit for a few minutes to cool (approximately 50ËšC) so it doesn't cook the eggs. Add the egg yolks and continue to whisk by hand. Set aside. To create the meringue, add the fresh egg white and inverted sugar to the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a whisk attachment. Mix on a slow speed until it reaches a firm peak. Using a spatula, slowly fold the meringue into the caramel mixture in three intervals. Once combined, transfer to a piping bag fitted with a 12mm round piping tip. Line a baking tray with the Flexipan. Pipe 90g of the mixture into each crevice of the mould in a swirling motion. Arrange pecans around the edge of the sponge. Bake at 170ËšC for 12 minutes. Allow to cool.


Pecan crunchy sable

200g unsalted butter

140g rapadura sugar

240g gluten free flour mix (above)

260g pecan powder

50g Callebaut Mycryo cocoa butter

50g Callebaut 823NV milk couverture 33.6%

METHOD

Soften the butter in a microwave and place in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment. Add in the sugar and mix until you achieve a smooth, creamy texture.

Stop the mixer and add in the pecan powder and gluten free flour mix. Mix to combine, but do not overmix. Remove the bowl from the mixer. Place half of the dough between two sheets of baking paper and roll out to 3.8mm with a rolling pin. You can also use a dough sheeter if you have one. Place in the freezer until cold, this will take approximately 20 minutes in a domestic freezer or 5 minutes in a blast freezer. Remove the baking paper and cut out two discs with a 16cm ring. Cut out a 7cm ring from the centre of the crunchy disc. Line a baking tray with a Silpain mat and place the discs on top. Bake at 140ËšC for 30 minutes. Place the scraps of dough together, cover in plastic wrap and keep in the fridge for another recipe. Melt the cocoa butter and chocolate together in the microwave in 30 second increments. Carefully brush a thin layer of the mixture on top of the discs. Allow to set in the fridge or freezer.


Passion fruit felly

0.5u Heilala vanilla bean, split and scraped

160g Ravifruit passionfruit puree

80g Ravifruit banana puree

80g Ravifruit mango puree

16g fresh passionfruit seeds

8g cornflour/ corn starch

32g gelatine mass(1:6)

METHOD

Place the vanilla seeds, purees, fresh passionfruit and cornflour in a saucepan and bring to a boil, while whisking by hand. Ensure you don't cook it. Once it starts boiling, remove from the heat and transfer to a jug. Mix in the gelatine mass with a spatula before emulsifying with a stick blender.

Pour 160g of the jelly into the smaller insert of the silicon mould. Cut out a 9cm ring from the centre of the cooled caramel sponge. Place the larger ring of sponge on top of the passionfruit jelly (pecan side up) and press down slightly. Place in the domestic freezer for 1 hour or 15 minutes in the blast freezer. You can store any leftover sponge in the freezer to utilise in another recipe.


Soft caramel

100g liquid glucose

60g caster (superfine) sugar

210g fresh cream 35% fat

2.5g sea salt

55g unsalted butter

15g Callebaut Mycryo cocoa butter

15g Callebaut gold couverture 30.4%

METHOD

Heat the glucose in the microwave slightly then add to a saucepan. Add the sugar to the saucepan and heat until you achieve the desired colour of brown-light brown (the temperature should be between 180 ËšC and 185ËšC). Heat the cream in the microwave for approximately 90 seconds (it will differ between microwaves, so ensure you check it).

Slowly add the hot cream to the caramel, being careful of the hot steam. Whisk by hand until the sugar has completely dissolved then add the salt and mix again to combine. Transfer the mixture to a jug and cool to 50ËšC. Add the gold chocolate and cocoa butter and mix with a spatula before adding in the cubed butter. Emulsify with a stick blender until shiny and smooth.

Transfer the caramel to a depositor and pour 50g on top of the frozen sponge.Move the mould to spread out the caramel. Place in the freezer so the caramel sets.

NOTEï¼šYou will have leftover soft caramel which can be used in another recipe.

250g fresh cream 35% fat

25g caster (superfine) sugar (B)

60g egg yolks

38g gelatine mass ï¼ˆ1:6ï¼‰

METHOD

Heat the sugar (A) in a saucepan to create a caramel. Heat the cream in the microwave for approximately 90 seconds (it will differ between microwaves so ensure you check it). Add in the hot cream, being careful of the hot steam, and whisk to combine. Remove from the heat and set aside.

Whisk the sugar (B) and egg yolks in a bowl by hand. Pour a small amount of the hot caramel into the yolk and whisk to combine, before transferring the mixture back into the saucepan. Return the saucepan to the heat and cook like an anglaise to between 82ËšC and 85ËšC, stirring constantly. Be careful not to boil the mixture. Transfer the caramel to a jug and add in the gelatine. Whisk to combine. When it has fully melted, cool down in the fridge to 50ËšC, this will take approximately 10-15 minutes.When the caramel has cooled, add the mascarpone and emulsify with a stick blender until smooth.

Tonka rice pudding

100g fresh cream 35% fat

400g full cream milk

3g tonka bean

25g caster (superfine) sugar

50g arborio rice

50g Callebaut gold couverture 30.4%

METHOD

Combine the milk and cream in a bowl and grate in the tonka bean. Cover and infuse for 24 hours. Strain the mixture into a saucepan and add the sugar. Bring the mixture to a boil, whisking occasionally by hand. When it comes to a boil, add in the pre-rinsed rice and whisk together by hand. Leave for approximately 10-15 minutes for the rice to cook. Pour over the chocolate in a bowl and combine with a spatula. Pour the mixture into a tray with sides so it cools down quicker and cover with plastic wrap touching the surface. Cool in the fridge for a few hours. When the cremeux caramel has set, place 150g of rice pudding on top of the set cremeux caramel. Smooth out with a large angled palette knife. Freeze for a few hours.


Pecan mousse

2u Heilala vanilla bean

180g full cream milk

40g egg yolks

180g Callebaut gold couverture 30.4%

25g gelatine mass

50g pecan paste

301g fresh cream 35% fat

METHOD

Infuse the vanilla beans in the milk overnight. Place the infused milk with the vanilla in a saucepan and add the egg yolks. Cook to between 82ËšC and 85ËšC while whisking by hand.

Strain the hot mixture over the chocolate in a bowl. Add in the gelatine and whisk by hand to combine. Add the pecan paste and whisk again before emulsifying with a stick blender until smooth. Cool to 34ËšC - 35ËšC. Semi-whip the cream in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a whisk attachment. Slowly fold the cream into the mousse in 2-3 intervals. Finish by slowly whisking by hand.


Gold oil

100g grapeseed oil

50g metallic gold powder

METHOD

Mix the oil and gold powder together with a stick blender.


Assembly

Unmould the frozen insert and pat down to remove any condensation.

Pour 350g of the pecan mousse into the larger mould. Remove the frozen insert from the fridge and place it on top of the mousse (flat side facing up). Press down gently until it is submerged in the mousse. Place the pecan crunchy disc on top, brushed side down. Place in the freezer for at least 3-4 hours.

Unmoldï¼Œglaze and decorate with chocolate.
