中英雙語·2020年諾貝爾文學獎獲得者詩人路易斯·格麗克介紹

2020-11-14
| 科技

Louise Glück, in full Louise Elisabeth Glück, (born April 22, 1943, New York, New York, U.S.), American poet whose willingness to confront the horrible, the difficult, and the painful resulted in a body of work characterized by insight and a severe lyricism.

露易絲·格麗克，美國詩人，1943年4月22日生於美國紐約州紐約市，以直面恐怖、困難和痛苦為主要特徵的作品，具有深刻的洞察力和強烈的抒情性。

After attending Sarah Lawrence College in Bronxville, New York, and Columbia University in New York City, Glück taught poetry at numerous colleges and universities, including Harvard and Yale. Her first collection of poetry, Firstborn (1968), used a variety of first-person personae, all disaffected or angry. The collection’s tone disturbed many critics, but Glück’s exquisitely controlled language and imaginative use of rhyme and metre delighted others. Although its outlook is equally grim, The House on Marshland (1975) shows a greater mastery of voice. There, as in her later volumes, Glück’s personae included historic and mythic figures such as Gretel and Joan of Arc. Her adoption of different perspectives became increasingly imaginative; for example, in “The Sick Child,” from the collection Descending Figure (1980), her voice is that of a mother in a museum painting looking out at the bright gallery. The poems in The Triumph of Achilles (1985), which won the National Book Critics Circle Award for poetry, address archetypal subjects of classic myth, fairy tales, and the Bible. These concerns are also evident in Ararat(1990), which has been acclaimed for searing honesty in its examination of the family and the self.

在紐約布朗克斯維爾的艾倫薩拉勞倫斯學院和紐約市的哥倫比亞沿大學就讀後，格麗克在許多學院和大學任教，包括哈佛大學和耶魯大學。她的第一部詩集：《長子》（1968），使用了各種第一人稱人物，充斥著不滿或憤怒。這一系列作品的基調令許多評論家感到不安，但格麗克精妙的語言和富有想象力的押韻和韻律的運用使他人感到愉悅。儘管她詩歌的前景同樣嚴峻，但《沼澤地上的房子》（1975年） 顯示出她更強大的話語控制能力。在那裡，正如在她後來的幾卷著作展示的一樣，葛呂克的作品角色包括歷史和神話人物，如格雷特和阿爾克碧恩。她採用不同的視角，想象力越來越豐富；例如，在選自作品集《走下坡路的人物》的作品《生病的孩子》中，她的詩歌語言藉助博物館中一副看著明亮的畫廊的母親的油畫。《勝利的阿喀琉斯》（1985）中的詩歌獲得了國家圖書評論家圈的詩歌獎，它講述了經典神話、童話和聖經的原型主題。對這些主體的關注在《阿拉拉特》（1990）中也很明顯，該書因其對家庭和自我的審視而備受讚譽。

In 1993 Glück won a Pulitzer Prize for The Wild Iris (1992). Her later works included Meadowlands (1996), The First Five Books of Poems (1997), and The Seven Ages (2001). Averno (2006) was her well-received treatment of the Persephone myth. The poems collected in A Village Life (2009)—about existence in a small Mediterranean town—were written in a lavishly descriptive style that significantly departed from the parsimony that characterizes her earlier verse. Poems 1962–2012 (2012) compiled all her published volumes of poetry. Faithful and Virtuous Night (2014) deals with mortality and nocturnal silence, sometimes from a male perspective; it won the National Book Award.

1993年，格麗克因《野生鳶尾花》（Wild Iris 1992）獲得普利策獎樂隊獎。她後來的作品包括1996年的《草地》，1997年出版的第一部五卷本詩集，以及2001年出版的的《七個時代》。2006年出版的《埃夫諾》 是描述珀耳塞福涅（希臘神話中的女子）的詩歌，該作品受到一致好評。收集在《鄉村生活歌》（2009）中的詩歌是關於地中海小鎮的生存狀態的詩歌，該作品以奢華的描寫風格加以呈現，明顯不同於她早期詩歌的簡約風格。她於2012年出版的《1962-2012詩選》收集了她發表的所有詩歌；她於2014年出版的《忠誠與貞潔之夜》描寫了死亡和靜謐之夜，有時她的詩歌從男性的角度看待世界，該作品獲得了全國圖書獎。

Poems by Louise Glück 露易絲·格麗克詩選

1

Child waking up in a dark room

screaming I want my duck back, I want my duck back

in a language nobody understands in the least —

There is no duck.

But the dog, all upholstered in white plush —

the dog is right there in the crib next to him.

Years and years — that's how much time passes.

All in a dream. But the duck —

no one knows what happened to that.

孩子在黑暗的房間裡醒來

尖叫著：我要我的鴨子回來

我要我的鴨子用一種沒有人能聽懂的語言回來

根本就沒有鴨子

但是那隻狗

全身披著白色毛絨軟墊的狗

就在他旁邊的嬰兒床裡安睡

年復一年

時間就這樣過去了

一切都在夢中

但是鴨子-沒人知道發生的一切

2

They've just met, now

they're sleeping near an open window.

Partly to wake them, to assure them

that what they remember of the night is correct,

now light needs to enter the room,

also to show them the context in which this occurred:

socks half hidden under a dirty mat,

quilt decorated with green leaves —

the sunlight specifying

these but not other objects,

setting boundaries, sure of itself, not arbitrary,

then lingering, describing

each thing in detail,

fastidious, like a composition in English,

even a little blood on the sheets —

他們剛認識

現在就睡在一扇開著的窗戶旁邊

一部分是為了喚醒他們

為了讓他們確信他們記憶中的夜晚是正確的

現在光線需要湧入房間

同時也要向他們展示發生故事的背景

襪子一半藏在髒墊子之下，被子用綠葉予以裝飾

陽光指定這些而不是其他物體，為它們設定邊界

把握命運，自由地活著

然後任由日子揮之不去，詳細描述每件事

生命需要講究，像工整的英文作文

甚至我們無法忽略床單上的一絲血跡

3

Afterward, they separate for the day.

Even later, at a desk, in the market,

the manager not satisfied with the figures he's given,

the berries moldy under the topmost layer —

so that one withdraws from the world

even as one continues to take action in it —

You get home, that's when you notice the mold.

Too late, in other words.

As though the sun blinded you for a moment.

後來，他們分開一天

甚至後來，在辦公桌上，在市場上

經理對他給出的數字不滿意

最上層的漿果發黴了

所以即使在繼續採取行動的時候

也會離開這個世界——你回家了

就是你發現黴菌的時候

換句話說，一切太晚了

因為雖然陽光讓你有一瞬間目空一切
